# Hindalco Industries — dossier

## TL;DR (≤ 6 lines)

- Aditya Birla Group flagship non-ferrous metals producer; world's largest single-location Cu smelter (Birla Copper Dahej, 500 ktpa cathode, expanding +300 ktpa) and parent of Novelis (92.5 % stake, US FRP leader).
- Entered downstream Cu tube (LWC + IGT for ACR) in 2024–25 via Waghodia, Gujarat plant: 25 ktpa Phase 1 → 50 ktpa Phase 2 → possible 75 ktpa; investment ₹450 cr for P1, ₹1,500 cr for expansion. India's first backward-integrated IGT plant.
- Environmental-clearance public hearing was Nov 2023, official plant announcement Sept 2024, LinkedIn/press inauguration April 2025 — **not 2023 as sometimes stated**.
- Won PLI White Goods (ACs + LED) in Round 3 (Jan 2025) and raised committed investment to ₹899 cr for Al + Cu AC components.
- Copper-tube technology vendor is **not publicly disclosed**; SMS Group has a confirmed Hindalco relationship but only for the aluminium cold mill at Lapanga Odisha (Sept 2022), not Waghodia.
- FY25 full-year revenue ₹238,496 cr (~USD 27.9 bn); Cu EBITDA ₹777 cr Q4, all-time high; sales crossed 500 kt for the first time.

## Products & positioning

### Copper (focus lens)

- **Cathodes** — LME A-grade, 99.99 % Cu from Dahej (Mitsubishi Process flash-smelting).
- **Continuous-cast rod** (1.35–4.5 mm wire-rod feedstock); world's #2 Cu rod producer.
- **Oxygen-free rod**, specialty alloy rods (traction, rail, EV).
- **Inner-grooved copper tube (IGT)** — Waghodia; "oxygen <10 ppm, eccentricity <5 %", LWC + plain pancake coils; 50 ft / 100 ft coils or ≤6 m straight; 100 % on-line ECT per ASTM E243 / ISO 9303 equivalent.
- **Plain smooth Cu tube** for ACR installation / connection kits.
- **Battery-grade Cu foil** — 11.5 ktpa Phase 1 at Waghodia for EV, BESS, industrial.
- **Precious metals** — Au, Ag 99.9 % as smelter by-products.
- **Cu recycling** — Dahej e-waste + Cu recycle facility, 50 ktpa initial, scalable to 200 ktpa (Metso technology partner per RecyclingToday).

### Aluminium / Novelis

- Primary aluminium (~1.3 mtpa), rolled products, extrusions, foils, specialty alumina.
- Novelis: flat-rolled Al, beverage-can sheet, automotive body sheet, aerospace — recycling-heavy circular model.

## Technology & automation posture

### Copper-tube route at Waghodia (inferred, **not vendor-confirmed**)

- Hindalco states "continuous Cast and Roll (C&R) technology involving melting, casting, surface peeling, rolling, drawing, annealing, grooving, and bright annealing" — matches SMS **directube®** architecture family (horizontal continuous casting of mother tube from cathode + planetary rolling + combined drawing + spinner block + LWC + grooving head + bright anneal).
- Public sources **do not name** the equipment OEM. Plausible suppliers for a greenfield 25 ktpa IGT plant:
  - SMS group (directube + VNVG 2000 grooving head — the reference for >30 k-rpm IGT spinner heads);
  - Senbo (China, lower-cost turnkey); or
  - Golden Dragon / Hailiang-sourced line under tech-transfer.
- SMS has a **confirmed** Hindalco contract — but it is for the **170 kt/yr aluminium cold mill at Aditya Aluminium Lapanga, Odisha** (announced 14 Sept 2022, commissioning end-2024). This is not Waghodia copper.
- Hindalco R&D: Aditya Birla Science & Technology Co. (ABSTCPL) Cu arm; 560+ patents granted in FY24-25 group-wide; Hindalco Innovation Centre.

### ERP / MES / L2

- SAP footprint referenced in annual reports (FY04, FY06 onwards) — likely SAP ERP / S4 HANA.
- No public naming of L2 process-control vendor at Waghodia.
- Dahej smelter uses Mitsubishi Process flash-smelting licence (Mitsubishi Materials).

## Competitive position

### Direct competitors

- **India Cu tube:** Mehta Tubes (Mexflow), Uniflow, Munot Metalloys, and importers of Hailiang / Mettube (MY) / Golden Dragon (CN) / KME / Wieland. With Waghodia, Hindalco becomes India's **largest** IGT maker and the only **backward-integrated** player (cathode → rod → tube under one roof).
- **India Cu cathode:** Hindustan Copper (PSU, vertically integrated with own mine), Adani's Kutch Copper (new 500 ktpa smelter commissioned Mar 2024).
- **Al / FRP:** Vedanta (Balco, Sterlite), NALCO (state-owned); globally Novelis competes with Constellium, Aleris-now-Novelis, Kaiser, Arconic.
- **Battery-grade Cu foil:** SKC-FR, Iljin Materials, Wason, Kingboard — all imports into India.

### Differentiators (verified, not marketing)

- **Only** Indian integrated cathode-to-tube player (verified: no other Indian producer operates both a primary smelter and a downstream IGT line).
- World's largest single-location Cu smelter (verified via multiple sources including Aditya Birla's own + Mitsubishi Materials licence page).
- Deepest domestic raw-material moat → tariff-proof vs Chinese IGT imports, favourable under PLI scheme reward formula.

## Red flags & risks

1. **Technology-vendor opacity at Waghodia.** For a 25 ktpa IGT plant to hit world-class yield (Hailiang's 5th-gen cast-and-roll benchmark: 93 % yield, −300 kWh/t) you need either SMS directube + VNVG 2000 or comparable Chinese line. Hindalco has not named the vendor publicly. If the OEM is second-tier, ramp-up and IGT fin-tolerance will be a risk — especially on heat-transfer-critical ACR tubes where OEMs (Daikin, LG) have strict spec.
2. **Novelis IPO deferred twice (May 2024, Oct 2024 withdrawal).** USD 750 mn share subscription from AV Minerals in 2025 plugs the gap but signals capital-structure pressure.
3. **Dahej 300 ktpa Cu smelter expansion** creates massive cathode overhang relative to in-country downstream draw. Hindalco must either vertically integrate further (tubes, foil, rod for EV) or export — currently the only announced downstream pull is ~37 ktpa (25 tubes + 11.5 foil).
4. **Regulatory overhang** — Dahej area sits in a high-pollution zone; any future SPM / SOx non-compliance could echo the 2013 Vedanta Tuticorin-style risk (not same company, but illustrative risk class). EC capacity at Waghodia was cleared at 7 400 MTA, which does not match the 25 ktpa announced — phase-wise re-clearance will be required.
5. **Force-Majeure gas-supply episode Mar 2026** — although ring-fenced (<0.1 % of ops), it exposes dependency on piped gas; extrusion pigments value chain risk.
6. **PLI claw-back risk** — Round 3 conditions require meeting investment + turnover thresholds by FY29; delays on Waghodia Phase 2 could cost PLI benefits.
7. **OT cybersecurity posture unpublished.** No IEC 62443 / ISO 27001 public claim — unusual for an SAP-footprint metals major.

## Opportunities if we engage

### As a customer (buy Cu tube / foil from Hindalco)

- First backward-integrated Indian supplier → short lead-time, no LME pass-through surprises vs import-heavy comps.
- ACR tube PLI-eligible → OEM AC customers can bank PLI benefits on BoM using Hindalco tubes.
- Tie-up on long-term supply during Phase 1 ramp can secure preferential pricing ahead of Phase 2 (25 → 50 → 75 ktpa) tightening.

### As a partner (automation / L2 / MES / AI)

- Waghodia is a greenfield — L2 and MES architecture likely open. ABG's SAP preference is strong but edge-AI / yield-optimisation (e.g., ECT signal-ML, drawing schedule optimisation) is not yet locked.
- Dahej smelter expansion = classic SMS / Outotec / Metso / ABB battle — copper-foil line vendor selection is a gating item (Ube / Mitsubishi Shindoh / Gold Leaf are the reference foil-makers).
- R&D centre (ABSTCPL) open to patent-co-development on grooving-head and bright-anneal optimisation (Hindalco filed 560+ patents in FY24-25).

### As a competitor

- Any non-Indian Cu tube producer (Hailiang, Mettube, Golden Dragon, KME, Wieland) loses its India import route. Mitigation: on-shore via JV or local finishing.

### As an M&A counter-party

- Novelis spin-out (IPO path 2025–26) creates a pure-play aluminium float — catalyst event for re-rating of residual Hindalco.
